Primary goal: Extract every quantitative or categorical detail that the article (main text + figures + footnotes + supporting information) states for each distinct photocatalytic experiment, and output it in the prescribed MediaWiki + CSV format. Core extraction policy (supersedes older, overly restrictive rules):

1. Sources  
   • Treat the entire published record that accompanies the article – main PDF, embedded tables/figures, footnotes, and the official Supporting Information (SI) – as equally valid.  
   • Use data that are legible and explicitly labelled; do not invent or guess.

2. Unit normalisation – always convert when the basis is explicit.  
   cat conc        → µM (1 mM = 1000 µM)  
   PS conc         → mM  
   e-D conc, H-D conc → M  
   Temperature     → °C (convert from K by subtracting 273.15)  
   λexc            → nm (use the centre wavelength for a band or a list for multi-LED arrays)  
   irr time        → h  (minutes ÷60 ; seconds ÷3600)  
   Turnover_frequency__* → h-1 (compute = TON/irr time when not directly reported)  
   intensity       → sun equivalents when light power is reported in mW cm-2 (1 sun = 100 mW cm-2) or as stated (e.g. “1 sun”)  
   Quantum_yield__* → % (multiply fraction by 100)

3. Calculation guidance  
   • If both TON and irradiation time are present, supply TOF = TON ÷ time (same product).  
   • If a frequency is given per minute or per second, convert to h-1.  
   • If light intensity is given in W m-2 or mW cm-2, convert to suns (divide by 100 mW cm-2). Keep numeric precision ≤3 sig. figs.  
   • Never derive TON from yield, selectivity, or curves—only from explicitly printed TON or from n(product)/n(cat).  
   • Only compute quantum yield when absorbed-photon or incident-photon data are explicitly supplied.

4. Text-field consistency  
   catalyst, PS, e-D, H-D, solvent, additives, feedstock gas: write exactly as the article spells them; preserve hyphens, Greek letters, oxidation-state numerals, superscripts, and spaces.  
   solvent-ratio: use “A:B” (e.g., “4:1”).  
   feedstock gas: record the charged gas before reaction (e.g., “CO2”, “CO2/Ar 10:90”).  
   additives: capture buffer salts, bases, acids, co-catalysts, headspace gases, or pressure if described as part of the reaction condition.  
   additives conc: same unit that the paper gives (M, mM, bar, equiv.); abbreviate unit with value but omit spaces, e.g., “0.1 M”.

5. Table-row policy  
   • One row = one explicitly described catalytic experiment (changed catalyst, PS, solvent, additive, light, gas, or time means a new row).  
   • Include control experiments and dark controls if their TON/TOF/quantum data are reported.  
   • Do not merge rows.  
   • If the paper states that multiple independent runs gave identical numbers, enter just one representative row.  
   • Leave a cell empty ONLY if the article provides no explicit value and no lawful calculation is possible.

6. Common pitfalls to avoid  
   • Do not swap catalyst vs. photosensitizer.  
   • Do not place base (e.g., TEOA) in solvent fields when it is primarily a sacrificial donor or additive; use e-D or additives appropriately.  
   • Distinguish “MeCN/H2O 4:1” (mixed solvent) from “H2O (buffered)” (single solvent + additive buffer).  
   • Ensure that TOF ≪ TON numerically; double-check for second ↔ hour mix-ups.  
   • If intensity converts to ~1 sun, write “1”, not “100”.
